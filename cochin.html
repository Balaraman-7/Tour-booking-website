ABOUT COCHIN

  • Kochi, formerly Cochin, is a major port city in Kerala on India’s southwest coast, known as the “Queen of the Arabian Sea.”
  • Its urban area includes Ernakulam, Fort Kochi, Mattancherry, Willingdon Island and many nearby islands and backwaters.
  • Kochi has a rich history of spice trade, with interactions over centuries with Arab, Chinese, Portuguese, Dutch and British traders.
  • Some famous historic landmarks include the Chinese Fishing Nets, St. Francis Church (one of India’s oldest European churches), Mattancherry Palace and the Paradesi Synagogue in Jew Town.
  • Kochi is culturally diverse – many religions, communities and ethnicities live here, reflected in its architecture, food and festivals.
  • Hill Palace in Tripunithura is Kerala’s largest archaeological museum, formerly the royal residence, set in a large estate with gardens and deer park.
  • Marine Drive is a popular promenade along Kochi’s backwaters, offering relaxing views, shopping and evening strolls for locals and tourists alike.
